Impact Advisors lens · Improve Business Performance

What this service line actually does

Helps healthcare organizations improve the full patient-to-payment process: patient access, registration, billing, claims, denials, collections, reimbursement performance, cash acceleration, patient financial experience, and margin improvement. In an Impact Advisors-style lens, this work combines operations, technology, analytics, and change adoption so fixes are not just identified, but implemented and sustained.

Accelerate cashReduce denialsImprove patient financial experienceOptimize EHR-driven workflows
What consultants look for
  • Where revenue leakage occurs
  • How claims and denials move through the system
  • Which process, payer, data, or technology issues are slowing cash

Impact Advisors lens · Improve Business Performance

What this service line actually does

Focuses on reducing non-labor costs and improving how supplies, vendors, contracts, inventory, preference cards, procurement, and technology support care delivery. The work is highly operational: consultants look for savings, process standardization, stronger analytics, cleaner item data, and better purchasing discipline without disrupting clinical teams.

Non-labor cost reductionProcure-to-pay automationInventory managementAnalytics-enabled savings
What consultants look for
  • Item master and vendor standardization
  • Inventory, par, and stockout issues
  • Contract compliance and supply expense opportunities

What this service line actually does

Helps hospitals and health systems improve quality, safety, outcomes, patient experience, and the accuracy of external quality ratings. Consultants in this space need to understand performance measures, regulatory programs, quality reporting, safety indicators, clinical variation, and how evidence-based process changes improve care delivery.

Quality ratingsPatient safetyOutcomes improvementRegulatory performance
What consultants look for

  • Which measures drive public ratings or reimbursement
  • How process, documentation, and clinical variation affect quality results
